Section Seven

From Insight to Action - The Three Principles

How the Pulse data validates the People Performance Q2-Q4 plan, and where it sharpens it.

Principle 1

Coaching for Performance

HP Workshops named the Coaching Gap in 11/15 teams. The Pulse confirms: 11% of staff want clearer leadership decisions and direction; 13% describe peak teams as trusting and supportive; 4% explicitly contrast "proactive" with "firefighting".

Pulse sharpens this: staff are not asking for more coaching frameworks - they are asking for leaders who say no to the next initiative, then coach the team through delivery of the current one.
Principle 2

Prioritisation

HP Workshops named the Prioritisation Trap in 13/15 teams. The Pulse confirms with the survey's loudest single ask: 19% want "less change at one time / time / capacity". Combined with 11% asking for clearer leadership decisions, this is the dominant signal.

Pulse sharpens this: the request is not "fewer projects in theory" - it is "close one stage before opening the next". Discipline of completion, not just selection.
Principle 3

Meetings

HP Workshops named meeting culture in 10/15 teams. The Pulse adds the staff voice: 20% want better communication, with explicit asks for fewer internal meetings, less virtual, more face-to-face, and meetings that produce decisions.

Pulse sharpens this: meetings are not just a calendar problem - they are the proxy for whether leadership is making decisions. Charter discipline must include "decision made" as the success measure.

Where the Pulse adds something the Q2-Q4 Plan does not yet address

Gap 1

Reward & Recognition is louder than expected

Sceptical Q2 responses anchor to "extra work, same pay, smaller bonus". 8% Q4 ask for recognition. This is not in the 3 Principles. It needs a deliberate response - either a policy answer or an honest narrative.

Gap 2

D2D translation for the personal stake

12% are sceptical or unsure what D2D means for them. The Pulse exposes a comms gap - the ambition is known but the personal "what's in it for me" has not landed. Q2 staff video and roadshow needs to close this.

Gap 3

Fun, enjoyment, human-ness

7% Q4 ask for fun and wellbeing. Q3 best-team language is dominated by energy and morale. The plan is rigorous - it needs a deliberate human and joyful counterweight or the workforce will read it as more pressure.

The Verdict

The Pulse is not a new dataset. It is the all-staff voice confirming what the leadership teams already said in their own workshops. The Q2-Q4 plan is built on the right three principles. The Pulse asks us to do three additional things: address recognition and reward head-on, close the D2D personal-meaning gap with relentless human comms, and keep joy and energy as deliberate design choices, not afterthoughts. When AG Barr achieves Dare to Double, it will be because of who we became - not what we chased.
